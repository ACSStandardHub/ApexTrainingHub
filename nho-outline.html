Day 1 · Presence & Professionalism
Professional Presence
Format: Slide deck, paired self-assessment activity, group debrief
10:15 – 11:00 AM · 45 min
⭐
Module ObjectiveAgents understand the observable, measurable standards of professional presence at Apex — and can assess themselves against those standards. The Friendly ≠ Familiar distinction is named and felt, not just stated.
Presence Standards (10-Point)
  • Uniform: complete, pressed, name badge visible
  • Posture: upright, engaged — not leaning or slumped
  • Phone: personal device off desk and out of hand on post
  • Eye contact: acknowledged within 10 seconds of resident approach
  • Tone: professional regardless of familiarity with resident
  • Desk organization: log open, supplies staged, nothing personal visible
  • Expression: neutral-to-warm default — not flat, not performative
  • Energy: consistent across the shift — not just when busy
  • Language: no slang, no profanity, no venting about work on post
  • Exit: desk not left unattended without coverage confirmed
Friendly ≠ Familiar
  • Friendly: warm, consistent, professional — the same for every resident
  • Familiar: casual, exclusive, boundary-crossing — varies by relationship
  • The test: "Would I behave this same way with every resident — or only this one?"
  • Examples of familiar behavior: nickname use without invitation, sharing personal opinions, texting or calling residents, social media connection
  • Friendly ≠ Familiar reference card distributed — agent keeps throughout program
Paired Activity: Presence Self-Assessment (10 min)
Agents pair up and assess each other against the 10-point presence checklist. Each agent receives a score and identifies their top development area. Facilitator debriefs: "What was hardest to assess in your partner? What was hardest to hear about yourself?"

Day 1 · Operations
Core Operations
Format: Slide deck, desk walkthrough visual, site tour exercise
11:00 AM – 12:00 PM · 60 min
⭐
Module ObjectiveAgents understand the non-negotiable operational standards of every Apex shift: shift start protocol, pass-on requirements, desk standards, minimum tour count, and shift close. They practice building a basic tour route.
Shift Start Protocol
  • Arrive 10 minutes before your shift — not on time, early
  • Review full pass-on log before the outgoing agent leaves
  • Confirm: open items, pending packages, known resident concerns
  • Radio charged and on hand before post is assumed
  • Desk organized and log open before outgoing agent departs
Non-Negotiable Desk Standards
  • The desk is never unattended — it is a post, not a seat
  • Relief must be confirmed before stepping away for any reason
  • No personal food, personal calls, or personal device use on post
  • Minimum 2 site tours per shift — each documented in the shift log
  • Shift close: pass-on written, open items flagged, relief briefed
Tour Practice Exercise (15 min)
Using the site tour template, agents build a 3-stop tour route for a fictional property: entry point, common area, and amenity zone. Each agent or pair presents their route and explains what they would document. Facilitator connects tour documentation to shift log requirements.

Day 1 · Shift Log Mastery
Shift Log Mastery — E.D.O.
Format: Slide deck, strong/weak comparison, live writing drill, peer scoring
12:45 – 2:00 PM · 75 min
⭐
Module ObjectiveAgents master the shift log as a formal operational record using the E.D.O. method (Event / Detail / Outcome). They understand why documentation quality is a primary performance indicator and can write compliant entries independently.
E
Event
What happened? Factual, observable, time-stamped. No editorializing.
D
Detail
Who was involved, what was said or done, what condition was observed. Specific enough to reconstruct.
O
Outcome
How was it resolved? Who was notified? What follow-up is required? Open items flagged.
7 Non-Negotiable Log Standards
  • Minimum one entry per hour — more during active periods
  • Every tour documented with time, route, and any observations
  • Pass-on is structured: open items, packages, follow-ups, notes for incoming agent
  • Language is factual and neutral — never editorial or emotional
  • Prohibited phrases: "quiet night," "nothing to report," "all good"
  • Corrections are addenda, not deletions — do not alter prior entries
  • Every interaction with management or maintenance is logged
Live Writing Drill (40 min)
  • Three scenarios read aloud by facilitator — agents write independently
  • No reference materials during the timed portion
  • Agents swap papers and score using the E.D.O. rubric (10-point)
  • Facilitator reveals model entries for each scenario
  • Group debrief: most common gaps and how to close them

Policies & Accountability
Format: Policy handout walkthrough, verbal call-out scenario, conduct acknowledgment
2:00 – 2:45 PM · 45 min
Attendance Policy
  • Call-out procedure: 5-step protocol — who to call, when, and what to say
  • Shift swaps: must be pre-approved, never assumed
  • No-call no-show: immediate formal documentation and review
  • Chronic tardiness: documented after second occurrence

Conduct Standards
  • Social media: no photos, no posts referencing residents, properties, or Apex
  • Personal calls: off desk — break or designated area only
  • Confidentiality: resident information is never shared — with anyone
  • Conflict: escalate to supervisor, do not engage or retaliate
  • Fraternal relationships: disclosed to management if it affects scheduling or operations

Day 1 · Scenarios
Real-World Scenarios — A.C.T.
Format: Six scenario drills, A.C.T. debrief, reference card distribution
3:00 – 4:00 PM · 60 min
⭐
Module ObjectiveAgents experience realistic on-shift situations and practice the A.C.T. de-escalation method. By end of module, they can apply A.C.T. to any resident interaction — including situations that escalate mid-conversation.
A
Acknowledge
Name the concern. Let the resident know they've been heard before anything else is said.
C
Control
Manage the environment, your tone, and the pace. Slow down. Step aside if needed. Don't match escalation.
T
Transfer or Resolve
Either resolve within your authority and document — or escalate with a warm transfer and full documentation.
Six Scenario Drills
  • S1 Resident disputes package delivery — claims it was marked delivered but never received
  • S2 Unauthorized person requests access to a resident's unit without notice or PTE
  • S3 Resident complaint about noise from neighboring unit — escalating in tone
  • S4 Vendor shows up without scheduled access — insists they are expected
  • S5 Resident asks agent personal questions and attempts to exchange contact information
  • S6 Agent observes a safety concern in a common area — no supervisor immediately reachable

Day 2 · Incident Documentation
Incident Report Documentation
Format: Field guide review, documentation drill — 3 scenarios, peer review
10:15 – 11:15 AM · 60 min
7 Situations Requiring an Incident Report
  • Any resident injury or medical event on property
  • Property damage — observed or reported
  • Unauthorized access attempt or breach
  • Physical or verbal altercation on property
  • Package loss, theft, or damage claim
  • Safety hazard observed (unremediated after 30 min)
  • Any interaction with police, fire, or EMS on property
Language Standard
  • "Resident appeared agitated" ✗ → "Resident raised voice, stated: 'This is unacceptable'" ✓
  • "The package was probably stolen" ✗ → "Package logged at 14:22. Not located at 18:00 check. Supervisor notified." ✓
  • "I think maintenance left the door open" ✗ → "Stairwell door observed propped open at 15:10. Closed by agent. Logged." ✓
  • Report what was observed, said, or done — not what was assumed or felt

Day 2 · Emergency Response
Emergency Response Protocols
Format: Reference card distribution, verbal quick-drill (no cards)
11:15 AM – 12:00 PM · 45 min
Three Protocol Cards (6 Steps Each)
  • Fire: Alert → Evacuate → Call 911 → Meet at assembly → Contact supervisor → Document
  • Medical: Assess → Call 911 → Do not move → Stay present → Contact supervisor → Document
  • Security: Do not engage → Alert supervisor → Call 911 if immediate threat → Observe and document → Meet emergency services at entry
Verbal Quick-Drill (10 min)

  • Laminated card remains at desk as a posted resource after program

Day 2 · Shift Log Practicum
Shift Log Practicum — Timed Assessment
Format: 40-min independent write, peer scoring, model entry review
3:30 – 4:30 PM · 60 min
⭐
Assessment StandardThis is assessment-level work. No assistance, no reference materials. Three scenarios read aloud — agents write full E.D.O. entries independently. Collect all forms before agents leave Day 2. Score and return written feedback before Day 3 begins.
Three Practicum Scenarios
  • Scenario A: Package dispute with a resident who becomes verbally aggressive — resolved at desk, supervisor notified
  • Scenario B: Maintenance request submitted during tour — work order placed, resident informed, follow-up flagged
  • Scenario C: Unknown individual accessed amenity space without key fob — observed, documented, management alerted
E.D.O. Scoring Rubric (10 pts)
  • Event present and time-stamped: 0–2 points
  • Detail complete — specific, factual, reconstructable: 0–3 points
  • Outcome documented — action taken, who notified, follow-up: 0–3 points
  • Language standard — neutral, factual, no editorializing: 0–2 points
  • Score of 7+ = compliant. Below 7 = remediation plan before solo shifts.
✍️
Collect All Forms Before Agents Leave Day 2Score overnight. Provide written feedback. Return scores at the start of Day 3 before Module 1 begins. Agents below 7/10 receive a remediation note — not disqualification.
