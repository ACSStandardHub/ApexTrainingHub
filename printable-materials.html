The Apex Standard — Five Non-Negotiables
  • Consistency — The same level of professionalism at 8 AM, at midnight, and on a slow Tuesday.
  • Intentionality — Nothing about how you present, communicate, or operate is accidental.
  • Ownership — You do not wait to be corrected. You hold the standard because it is yours.
  • Representation — You are Apex on shift. You are also Apex near the property off shift.
  • Non-Negotiable — The standard does not lower based on volume, resident behavior, or how your day is going.
"What you walked into this morning — the quiet, the order, the intentionality — that is what Apex-level preparation looks like."

Friendly ≠ Familiar — Professional Boundary Reference
Friendly — Required
  • Warm, engaged greeting by name
  • Genuine interest in resident wellbeing
  • Proactive offers of assistance
  • Professional warmth — every resident, every time
Familiar — Never
  • Nicknames, casual address, favoritism
  • Personal conversations beyond the role
  • Sharing opinions on internal matters
  • Special treatment based on relationship
"Would I behave the same way with every resident — or only this one?"
If the answer is only this one — you have crossed into familiar. Friendly is consistent. Familiar is selective.

Shift Expectations One-Pager
Shift Start — Before You Sit Down
  • Arrive 10 minutes before your shift — never clock in and immediately take position unprepared.
  • Review the pass-on log in full before the outgoing agent leaves.
  • Confirm open items: pending packages, active maintenance, known resident concerns.

  • The desk is never unattended. Relief before stepping away — always.
  • Minimum 2 property tours per shift — both logged using E.D.O.
  • Minimum 1 shift log entry per hour — time-stamped.
  • Complete pass-on entry required at end of every shift — all three sections.

D1-09 · Module 5D🖨 Print
Shift Log Non-Negotiables
  • Minimum 1 entry per hour — every entry time-stamped. If nothing occurred, log that explicitly.
  • Minimum 2 tour entries per shift — route taken, stops covered, observations noted.
  • Pass-on entry required at end of every shift — all three sections completed.
  • Tone: professional, neutral, factual — no personal opinion, no casual language, no first person.
  • "Quiet night" and "Nothing to report" are not acceptable entries — ever.
  • Corrections: never delete — add a follow-up entry with updated information and timestamp.
  • Never copy and paste previous shift entries — every entry must reflect the actual shift.
Prohibited Language in the Log
Never Write"Things were quiet tonight."
"Handled it."
"The resident was drunk."
"I think it was fine."
Write This"No incidents to report — 1:05 AM tour completed, all areas clear."
"[Specific action taken and outcome documented.]"

Attendance & Conduct Policy Handout
Call-Out Procedure — 5 Required Steps
  • Call out minimum 2 hours before your shift begins — no exceptions.
  • Text AND call — do not rely on a single form of communication.
  • Contact direct supervisor AND the on-call manager — both.
  • Provide a reason and estimated return-to-duty status.
  • If absence exceeds 2 consecutive days: return with documentation.
If you do not follow this procedure, the absence is unexcused — regardless of the reason.
Shift Swap Protocol
  • Attempt direct coverage swap first. Post in All Users Chat if needed.
  • Management confirmation required before any swap is official.
  • A verbal agreement between agents is not a confirmed swap.
  • You remain responsible for the shift until management releases you.
Conduct Standards
  • No photos, videos, or commentary about residents or the property on social media.
  • Personal calls kept to emergencies during working time.
  • Conflicts with coworkers: handled privately with supervisor — never at the desk.

D1-14 · Module 7 Start🖨 Print
Escalation Decision Tree
Can I resolve this within my role and authority?
Yes — Resolve
  • Take action within your role scope
  • Log the event using E.D.O.
  • Note any follow-up required in pass-on
No — Escalate
  • Contact supervisor immediately
  • Provide: what happened, who is involved, what you have done so far
  • Log the escalation — including time and who was notified
Always Log — Regardless of Path
  • Resident complaint escalated or unresolved at desk level → File + log
  • Security concern or suspicious person → Escalate + log
  • Any injury, damage, or altercation → Incident report + log
  • Policy gray area (no clear answer) → Escalate before acting, then log
"Escalation is strength. The agent who escalates correctly protects themselves, the resident, and the company."

D1-15 · Module 7 — After Scenarios🔖 Laminated
A.C.T. Method Reference Card
A
Acknowledge
Recognize what is happening. Do not react — respond. Let the resident know they have been heard before anything else.
C
Control
Control the environment first, then the conversation. Lower your voice. Create space. Your calm is your most powerful tool.
T
Transfer or Resolve
Resolve within your authority — or escalate. Document the action and outcome regardless of which path you take.
Common Failure Points
  • Skipping A: Jumping to solution before the resident feels heard creates more conflict.
  • Matching energy: When a resident raises their voice, the instinct is to match it. Do the opposite.
  • Over-promising in T: Only commit to what you can actually deliver.

D2-03 · Module 2 Start🖨 Print
Incident Report Field Guide
When to File — Always Required
  • Any physical altercation, threat, or confrontation — regardless of outcome
  • Any injury to a resident, guest, or visitor — even minor
  • Any property damage — structural, amenity, or unit-adjacent
  • Any unauthorized entry, security breach, or suspicious activity acted upon
  • Any medical emergency — including EMS response
  • Any resident complaint escalated or unresolved at desk level
When in doubt — file. An unnecessary report is recoverable. A missing report is not.
Required Fields — Every Report
  • Date and time — exact, to the minute
  • Location — specific: floor, unit vicinity, amenity name
  • Parties involved — full names or physical description
  • What happened — objective, sequential, factual only
  • Actions taken — who notified, when, what response
  • Outcome/status — resolved, pending, or escalated to whom
Language Standard
Never Write"The resident was drunk"
"He was being aggressive"
"The situation was handled"
Write This"Resident exhibited slurred speech and unsteady gait"
"Subject moved toward the desk with raised voice"
"Supervisor notified at 9:42 PM. Resolved by 9:55 PM."

D2-05 · Module 3 — Desk Posting🔖 Laminated
Emergency Response Reference Card
🔴 Fire
  • Call 911 immediately. Do not investigate.
  • Activate fire alarm if not already triggered.
  • Direct residents to nearest exit — no elevators.
  • Stage at assembly point. Do not re-enter.
  • Meet emergency responders at entrance.
  • Document: alarm time, your actions, who you notified.
🔵 Medical
  • Call 911. Provide address, floor, nature of emergency.
  • Do not move the individual unless immediate danger.
  • Stay with them — calm and professional. Clear bystanders.
  • Meet EMS at entry. Provide access and information.
  • Document: time, what was observed, who responded.
🟡 Security Threat
  • Do not confront. Maintain distance and calm demeanor.
  • Call 911 if there is a credible threat to safety.
  • Notify supervisor — use radio or secondary line.
  • Observe and document: description, direction, what was said.
  • Your safety is not secondary to the building's. Remove yourself first.
Call first. Document everything. Support within your role.

Prohibited Practices Reference
Package Handling
  • Storing any package without logging it first
  • Releasing a package without verifying recipient identity
  • Misclassifying high-value items as standard packages
  • Accepting outgoing packages without proper postage
Log & Documentation
  • Submitting vague entries ("quiet night", "nothing to report")
  • Deleting or editing entries — corrections are additions only
  • Logging after the shift ends instead of in real time
  • Using casual or editorial language in any log entry
Resident Interactions
  • Discussing residents with other residents
  • Sharing opinions on internal building or management matters
  • Making leasing or maintenance commitments on behalf of the property
  • Social media posts about residents, incidents, or the property
Site Conduct
  • Leaving the desk without secured coverage
  • Personal phone use visible to residents while at the desk
  • Accepting gifts, meals, or financial arrangements from residents
  • Personal relationships with residents outside the role scope
